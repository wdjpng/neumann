Dear Professor! Lately I have been occupied with the question of uniqueness of the solutions of the commutation relation PQ − QP = h2π i · 1, respectively with equations U(α) V(β) = ei αβ V(β) U(α) (U(α) = e(2π i / h) · αP,  V(β) = ei · βQ), and I have proved the uniqueness*) by a rather simple method. Since it is modeled on your treatment of continuous closed groups by means of the integral equations corresponding to the “momentum quantum numbers,” it may interest you, and I think I shall sketch it in the following. I introduce the unitary family S(α,β) = eiαβ/2 U(α) V(β) = eiαβ/2 V(β) U(α), for which the multiplication relations S(α,β) · S(γ,δ) = e(i/2)(αδ − βγ) · S(α+γ, β+δ) hold, and consider operators A = ∬ a(α,β) · S(α,β) dα dβ, with “kernel” a(α,β) absolutely integrable over the entire (α,β)-plane. It is easy to state the computational rules for these kernels; in particular, *(a(α,β) S′(α,β)) = ∬ a(−α,−β) · S′(α,β) dα dβ. (…) ∬ a(α,β) S*(α,β) dα dβ S′(μ,ν) = ∬ e i(ν α − μ β) a(α − μ, β − ν) · S(α,β) dα dβ, S(μ,ν) … = ∬ e − i(ν α − μ β) a(α − μ, β − ν) · S(α,β) dα dβ, ⊧ ∬ a(α,β) S*(α,β) dα dβ · ∬ b(α,β) S(α,β) dα dβ = ∬ C(α,β) S(α,β) dα dβ, where (formally) also l(α,β) = ∬ e½ i (β − α) a(ξ,η) · b(α − ξ, β − η) dξ dη. Moreover, from ⟨ ∬ a(α,β) S(α,β) dα dβ ⟩ = 0 and S(u,v)…S′(u,v) = 0, i.e. ∬ ei(…) a(α,β) S(α,β) dα dβ = 0 for all u,v, it follows that a(α,β) ≡ 0. Thus the calculus with the “kernels” is equivalent to that with their operators. Now I set A := ∬ e−¼ α² − ¼ β² · S1(α,β) dα dβ.
