Dear Professor Bernays!
So I am sending you the proof of the well-ordering of your ordering ≤, i.e., of
(∀x)(∃y) [ Ur(x) → (y ≤ x) ∧ Ur(y) ] → (∀x) Ur(x)1
for any proposition Ur, with free variable v, etc., without the use of propositional variables. For simplicity I have written the proof in words and have indicated only the important items—my definitions and the statements proved—formally. (The proof is on sheets 1–3; the formulas are appended on the sheet “Formulas”; references to them are numbered I. – XI.) The idea is this: V(x) has a first element with respect to ≺ if this holds for a sequence of other formulas under ≤v-1; these in turn have it if it holds for a further sequence of other formulas under ≤v-2; ... All these formulas form a sequence that I work out and specify in VII. The statement that these formulas have first elements at ξ [illegible] can therefore be proved on its own by induction and contains only basic variables. Where in IV. – XI. propositional variables seem to occur, they are communication variables that serve merely as abbreviations and could be avoided.
