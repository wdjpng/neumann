I do not know how to interpret the symmetry of the antipodes. The most radical solution would be always to identify two antipodes x1, x2, x3, …, xr and −x1, −x2, −x3, …, −xr. This operation (a partial analogue of which is indeed considered in Einstein’s cylinder world) would, however, have the effect that one could not fix a unique time direction Past → Future (for all world lines).

Rather, this direction would have to be defined once and for all for the world line of each atom. To remain in accord with causality one must declare: signals A → B (below the speed of light) are only possible if A lies in B’s past and B in A’s future. s future’s lies. Between world lines with opposite time directions there are thus no signals. For each world line both past and future encompass the whole hyperbolic world, so that of any other world line one part has the same, but the other an opposite, time direction. Thus distant heavenly objects will at first be visible here as well, recede ever faster and show ever stronger time dilation, and never be traceable beyond a certain time. On the star, of course, nothing happens; the observer will explain that first a standstill, then a reversal of time occurred.
