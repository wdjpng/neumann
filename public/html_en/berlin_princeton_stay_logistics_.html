Berlin, 24 Nov 1929.
Hs 91: 684

Dear Professor,

Please excuse me for thanking you only now for your last letter and for the information it contained. I have now definitively declined the offer from Baltimore.

I have just received a letter from O. Veblen in Princeton offering me a four-month stay in Princeton (for the semester 5 Feb 1930 – 1 Jul 1930), as he wrote, so that I might give lectures on quantum mechanics after your return to Zurich. The obligations are 2–3 hours per week; in return he offers \$ 4000 (including travel expenses).

I assume that this offer (considering American conditions) must be called advantageous, and I would be especially pleased and honored to come to the place where you worked for so many years.

I therefore intend to accept.

On this occasion I would also like to inform you that I became engaged in October to Miss Mariette Kövési of Budapest.—

At the moment I still do not quite see how I shall arrange the trip to America in view of these circumstances; but I believe it will in any case be possible.

Scientifically I have learned little that is new here. Einstein is said to have produced a reasonable version of his theory. What do you think of Reichenbächer, Z. f. Ph. 58 57?

With best wishes, yours very sincerely
J. v. Neumann.
