Dear Professor,

I have just received your letter and have sent Mr. Kaufmann the Kaufmann paper together with my comments. I trust that I have acted in accordance with your intentions; please excuse the fact that this has only happened now.

has now passed away.

I have done my best to formulate my objections as little brutally as possible; it was not easy, since Mr. Kaufmann forgets trifles such as the principle of complete induction and solves the continuum problem in \(7\tfrac{1}{2}\) pages!

I would not like to let this opportunity pass without expressing to you, Professor, my sincere thanks for the great goodwill you have shown me in the course of the prize-assignment affair. As I wish to trouble you as little as possible with the galley proofs, I shall perhaps pick them up in Zurich at the end of September.

I remain, respectfully,
Your most devoted,
