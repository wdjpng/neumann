THE INSTITUTE FOR ADVANCED STUDY PRINCETON, NEW JERSEY
Dec. 5.
Dear Mr. Alexandroff, Many thanks for your kind invitation to receive, in excerpt, my Moscow lectures. It so happens that I had already set down a manuscript of my Congress lecture in May 1935, which I intended to publish in the “Annals of Mathematics” under the title “The Unicity of Haar’s Measure.” Its length corresponds almost exactly to the maximum you specified (≈ 15 standard printed pages). I should therefore like to propose that—whichever you consider appropriate—I submit this work, in somewhat fuller form, either for publication as a “Congress lecture” or in the “Recueil Mathématique.” If that suits you, I could let you have the manuscript at once. My lecture before the Moscow Mathematical Society is rather more difficult; on that subject I am to prepare a longer paper whose completion will presumably still require some time. N. B. While I was in Moscow I accepted, at the kind invitation of Mr. Bérnart, to contribute a paper on “Measure Theory in Function Spaces” to the “Recueil Mathématique.” I hope that you have already recovered well from the labors of the Congress—for it must surely have been as arduous for you as it was delightful for us. We all look back with the greatest pleasure on those enchanting days that we owe to your hospitality. With best regards I remain, yours faithfully, J. v. Neumann
