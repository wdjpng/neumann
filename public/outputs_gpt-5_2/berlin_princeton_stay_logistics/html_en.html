Berlin, Kurfürstendamm 233, at Goldschmidt's.
Berlin, November 24, 1929.
Hs. 91: 684

Dear Professor!

Please excuse me for only now thanking you for your last letter and the information contained therein. I have definitively declined Baltimore.

I have just received a letter from O. Veblen in Princeton offering me a 4-month stay in Princeton (for the semester February 5, 1930 – July 1, 1930), specifically, as he informs me, to give lectures on quantum mechanics after your return to Zurich. The obligations are: 2–3 hours per week; he offers in return $4000 (incl. travel expenses).

I assume that this offer (considering American conditions) can be described as advantageous, and I would be particularly pleased and honored to come to the place where you worked for many years; consequently I intend to accept.

On this occasion I would also like to inform you that I became engaged in October, to Miss Mariette Kövi of Budapest. —

How I shall actually arrange the trip to America in view of these circumstances, I do not yet see at the moment, but I believe that it can in any case be managed.

Scientifically I have learned little new here. Einstein is said to have composed a new edition of his theory. What do you think of Reichenbächer, Z. f. Ph. 58, 573.

I am, with best regards, yours very truly

J. v. Neumann.
