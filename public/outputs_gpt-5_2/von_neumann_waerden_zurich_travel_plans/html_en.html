Dear van der Waerden:

Please excuse my delay in answering your very kind invitation of July 13--I have been travelling a good deal and was in Mexico during most of the last five weeks.

I would like to visit Zürich during my European trip next summer, but I am afraid that I won't be able to come there after the Congress. I expect that I will have to return then more or less directly (with at most a short stopover in England) to Princeton. I do expect to come to Zürich shortly before the Congress--but I am afraid that this may not be a very good time for seeing you or the other colleagues there or to give a talk, since this is likely to be your vacation period. Am I correct in this? If this is not the case, I would be more than delighted to stop in Zürich before the Congress and give a talk at this time, if this is agreeable to you.

With best personal regards,
Cordially yours,
J. von Neumann
John von Neumann.

Dear von Neumann
You are right that August-September is a vacation period here. The vacations begin about July 18. I am very sorry that a talk at our Colloquium is out of the question. However, I'll be delighted to see you personally. I'll be in town from Aug. 20 until the beginning of the Amsterdam congress. Yours most cordially.
