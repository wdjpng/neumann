N. G
Hs 91: 681
Budapest, 19.8.1928.
Dear Professor!

Together with this letter I take the liberty of sending you the preliminary clean copy of my work on Hermitian operators (which in this form is not intended for publication) — perhaps you will find some points of interest.

I believe I have quite thoroughly resolved the question of eigenvalue representation here with a simple and constructive method (insofar as that is at all possible) (Chapters III–VI). I would also like to mention that there exists a single operator (essentially) that admits no eigenvalue representation (Chapter VII, Appendix III), as well as the curious "pathology" of unbounded matrices (Chapter I, end of Appendix II).

Furthermore, I have the impression that the "eigenvalue-less" operator I found may play a role in the spinning electron — but at present I know little that is definite about this.

In the course of my deliberations I made an observation that seems to be unknown; perhaps you know something about it. Namely, the 2+1-dimensional Lorentz group is represented (albeit fractionally) one-to-one by the conformal mappings of the unit circle onto itself

\( y = \varepsilon \dfrac{x - a}{1 - \bar{a}\,x} \),   \( |\varepsilon| = 1,\quad |a| < 1 \)

This group now possesses an infinite-dimensional and irreducible representation. Namely:

To the point \(x_0, x_1, x_2, \ldots\) of the infinite-dimensional unitary (Hilbert) space \(\bigl(\sum x_n^{2}\ \text{finite!}\bigr)\) I assign the function

\( f(\xi) = \sum_{0}^{\infty} x_n\, \xi^{\,n} \)

The transformation

\( f(z) \longrightarrow \dfrac{\sqrt{e}\,\sqrt{\,1-a\bar{a}\,}}{1-a\bar{z}}\,f\!\left(\dfrac{z-a}{1-\bar{a}z}\right) \)

now carries these \( f(z) \) into the same kind, and is therefore a linear mapping of Hilbert space onto itself — specifically a unitary one. One easily shows the presence of the representation property, as well as the irreducibility.

Are there methods for controlling the infinite-dimensional irreducible representations of such a group?

Thanking you, also on behalf of my parents, for your kind wishes, I remain yours very respectfully

J. v. Neumann.
Budapest – Svábhegy, I/b. Eötvös út 15.
