Dear Professor Bernays!

Enclosed I send you the proof of the well-ordering of your order \( \underset{\small\vee}{<} \), i.e. of

\( (x)(E y)\big[\, U(x)\rightarrow \big((y \underset{\small\vee}{<} x)\land U(y)\big)\,\big]\;\rightarrow\; (x)\,\overline{U(x)}\,, \)

for every proposition \(U\), with free variable \(v\), i.e. without use of propositional variables. For simplicity's sake I have written out the proof in words, and only formally stated the most important intermediate formulas (definitions and proven propositions). (The proof is on sheets 1.-3., the formulas as appendix on each sheet; formulas that are referenced have numbers I. - XI.) The idea is this: \(U(x)\) has a first element for \(\le\) if this holds for a sequence of other formulas under \(\underset{\small v-1}{<}\), these in turn have it if it holds for a further sequence of other formulas under \(\underset{\small v-2}{<}\), ... All these formulas form a sequence, which are given in VII. The proposition that I work out and state in VII., the proposition that all these formulas have first elements under \( \underset{\small\vee}{<} \), can therefore be proven inductively by itself, and contains only basic variables. Where propositional variables seemingly appear in IV. – XI., they are communication variables that serve only as abbreviations and could be avoided. (It would be possible here to always replace the defined by the defining.) —

Please excuse that I didn't contact you again on Saturday; however, I still had to make 4 more visits. I would be very grateful if you would send my notes back at your convenience, as I have no copy.

With best regards Yours

J. v. Neumann.
