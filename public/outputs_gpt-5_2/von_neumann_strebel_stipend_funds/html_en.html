THE INSTITUTE FOR ADVANCED STUDY
PRINCETON, NEW JERSEY
SCHOOL OF MATHEMATICS
January 23, 1953
Dear van der Waerden: Thank you for your letter of January 18 concerning Dr. Kurt Strebel. His credentials look good to me, but our stipend funds for 1953-54 are already largely exhausted. It is, nevertheless, not impossible that we may be able to do something, but, because of the reason mentioned above, this is a good deal more uncertain than Dr. Strebel's objective qualifications should warrant. If we are unable to act favorably for 1953-54, would Dr. Strebel be interested in 1954-55? I am enclosing the Institute Application Blanks with this letter, and I am also circulating your and Professor Nevanlinna's letters to my colleagues. I am, with best regards,
Yours cordially,
John von Neumann
John von Neumann

Prof. Dr. B.L. van der Waerden Mathematisches Institut der Universität Zürich Zürich, GERMANY
[Handwritten reply on letter:]
Dear Von Neumann,
Many thanks for your letter of Jan. 23. Dr. Strebel has applied for a Swiss stipendium, and it seems that it will be granted. which will enable him to go to America. We have the impression that this will be granted. If not, he could perhaps apply for 1954/55. In any case I keep the application forms you sent me
Yours most cordially
