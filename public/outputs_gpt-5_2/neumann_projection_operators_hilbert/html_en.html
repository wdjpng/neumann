Hs 91: 685
Cortina d'Ampezzo, August 5
1930.
Dear Professor!
Recently I have been occupied with the uniqueness question of the solutions to the commutation relation
\( PQ - QP = \dfrac{h}{2\pi i}\,1 \)
or their equations \( U(\alpha)\,V(\beta) \;=\; e^{i\alpha\beta}\, V(\beta)\,U(\alpha) \)
( \(U(\alpha)=e^{\left(\frac{2\pi i}{h}\right)aP}\), \(V(\beta)=e^{\,i\beta Q}\) ), and have proven the uniqueness with a rather simple method*). Since it relates to your treatment of continuous closed groups with the help of integral equations corresponding to the "group parameters," it may perhaps interest you, and I permit myself to sketch it in the following. I introduce here the unitary family
$$S(\alpha,\beta)\;=\;e^{-\tfrac{i}{2}\alpha\beta}\,U(\alpha)V(\beta)\;=\;e^{\tfrac{i}{2}\alpha\beta}\,V(\beta)U(\alpha)$$
for which the multiplication relations read
\( S(\alpha,\beta)\; S(\gamma,\delta) \;=\; e^{\frac{i}{2}\,(\alpha\delta-\beta\gamma)} \; S(\alpha+\gamma,\;\beta+\delta) \)
and I considered operators
\( A = \iint_{-\infty}^{+\infty} a(\alpha,\beta)\, S(\alpha,\beta)\, d\alpha\, d\beta. \)
with thus (over the entire \( \alpha, \beta \)-plane) absolutely integrable "kernel" \( a(\alpha,\beta) \). It is easy to state the calculation rules for these kernels; in particular
$$\Bigg(\iint a(\alpha,\beta)\,S(\alpha,\beta)\,d\alpha\,d\beta\Bigg)^{*} \;=\; \iint \overline{a(-\alpha,-\beta)}\,S(\alpha,\beta)\,d\alpha\,d\beta.$$
$$\iint a(\alpha,\beta)\,S(\alpha,\beta)\,d\alpha\,d\beta \;\cdot\; \iint b(\alpha,\beta)\,S(\alpha,\beta)\,d\alpha\,d\beta$$ $$=\;\iint c(\alpha,\beta)\,S(\alpha,\beta)\,d\alpha\,d\beta$$
$$S(u,v)\,\iint \ldots \;=\; \iint e^{-\tfrac{1}{2} i \,(u\alpha - v\beta)}\, a(\alpha - u, \beta - v)\, S(\alpha, \beta)\, d\alpha\, d\beta$$
$$ S(u,v) \;=\; \iint e^{\,i\zeta\,(v\alpha - u\beta)}\, a(\alpha - u,\, \beta - v)\, S(\alpha,\beta)\, d\alpha\, d\beta $$
with \( L(\alpha,\beta) = \iint e^{\tfrac{i}{2}(\beta \xi - \alpha \eta)}\, a(\xi,\eta)\, b(\alpha-\xi,\beta-\eta)\, d\xi\, d\eta. \)
Furthermore, from \( \iint a(\alpha,\beta)\, S(\alpha,\beta)\, d\alpha\, d\beta = 0 \) it follows that
\( S(-u,-v)\; \int\int \ldots\; S(u,v)=0, \)
that is
$$ \iint e^{\,i(\alpha x-\beta t)}\; a(\alpha,\beta)\, S(\alpha,\beta)\, d\alpha\, d\beta \;=\; 0 $$
for all \(u,v\) — and from this easily \(a(\alpha,\beta)\equiv 0\). The calculus with the "kernels" is thus equivalent to that with their operators.
Now I set
\(A \;=\; \iint e^{-\tfrac{1}{4}\alpha^{2} - \tfrac{1}{4}\beta^{2}} \; S\, \alpha(\beta)\; d\alpha \, d\beta.\)
Then one recognizes through easy calculations
$$A \ne 0,\quad A = A^{*},\quad A\,S(u,v) = 2\pi\,e^{-\tfrac{1}{4}u^{2}-\tfrac{1}{4}v^{2}} A$$
Thus in particular \( \frac{1}{2\pi} A \) is a projection operator \( \neq0 \) \((u=v=0)\), let \(f\) be a solution of \( \frac{1}{2\pi}Af = f, |f|=1 \). All \( f_{\alpha,\beta} = S(\alpha,\beta)f \)
determine a space \( \omega \), which is invariant with respect to all \( S(\gamma,\delta) \), that is, all \( U(\gamma), V(\delta) \), and therefore (by irreducibility!) the full Hilbert space.
Now \( (f_{\alpha, \beta}, f_{\gamma, \delta}) = (S_{\alpha, \beta}f, S(\gamma, \delta)f) = \)
$$= \frac{1}{4\pi^{2}}\,( S(\alpha,\beta)Af,\; S(\gamma,\delta)Af ) \;=\; \frac{1}{4\pi^{2}}\,\big( A\,S(-\gamma,-\delta)\,S(\alpha,\beta)\,A f,\; f \big) =$$
$$= \frac{1}{2\pi}\, e^{-\tfrac{1}{4}(\alpha-\gamma)^{2}-\tfrac{1}{4}(\beta-\delta)^{2}+\frac{1}{2}i(\alpha\delta - \beta\gamma)}\qquad (Af,f)=e^{\ldots}\,(f,f)=$$
\[ e^{-\tfrac{1}{4}(\alpha-\gamma)^2 - \tfrac{1}{2}(\beta-\delta)^2 + \frac{1}{2}\,i(\alpha\delta-\beta\gamma)} \]
\[ S(u,v)\, f_{\alpha\beta} \;=\; e^{\tfrac{i}{2}\,(u\beta - v\alpha)}\; f_{\alpha+u,\;\beta+v} \]
Here H is the set of all accumulation points of all linear aggregates of any (finitely many) \(f,\ \alpha,\ \beta\).
But that two such H with properties I, II can be mapped one-to-one, isometrically and \(S(\alpha,\beta)\)-isomorphically onto each other is evident (let the \(f_{\alpha,\beta}\) correspond to each other, and extend linearly-continuously to all of \( \omega \) ).
The same method is also applicable to the multidimensional commutation relations, or their general theory of "up to a numerical factor Abelian" unitary operator groups.
Will I have the opportunity to see you again in Königsberg?
In the hope of seeing you again
I remain your very devoted
Johann v. Neumann
Budapest, V. Vilmos császár út 62.
